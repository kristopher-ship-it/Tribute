Stephen Hawking

The man who made a bigger step for mankind

A timeline of Stephen Hawking's remarkable life:

  • 1942
    HAWKING WAS BORN
    - Born in Oxford, England
  • 1959
    OXFORD UNIVERSITY
    - At only 17 years old, Hawking begins studying physics at Oxford University. Finding the work quite easy, Hawking studies very little. During his last year of study, Hawking becomes increasingly clumsy.
  • 1962
    FIRST-CLASS HONORS DEGREE
    - After receiving a first-class honors degree in natural science from Oxford, Hawking starts his graduate work at Trinity Hall, Cambridge.
  • 1963
    MEETS FUTURE WIFE
    - While attending a party, Hawking meets Jane Wilde, a languages undergraduate.
  • 1963
    DIAGNOSIS
    - Just shy of his 21st birthday, Hawking is diagnosed with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) â€“ a rare disease that affects movement and speech. He is told he is only likely to live for two more years.
  • 1965
    MARRIAGE
    - Jane and Stephen marry. They have three children together.
  • 1965
    THESIS
    - Hawking writes his thesis based on applying Roger Penrose's theory of space-time singularity.
  • 1966
    ADAMS PRIZE
    - Hawking's essay Singularities and the Geometry of Space-Time wins him the Adams Prize.
  • 1970
    DISCOVERS HOLE DYNAMICS
    - Hawking discovers The Second Law of Black Hole Dynamics. Using quantum theory and general relativity, Hawking reveals that black holes can emit radiation.
  • 1975
    GOLD MEDAL FOR SCIENCE
    - Pope Paul VI awards Hawking the Pius XI Gold Medal for Science.
  • 1977
    GRAVITATIONAL PHYSICS PROFESSOR
    - Hawking is appointed as professor of gravitational physics at Cambridge.
  • 1978
    HONORARY DOCTORATE
    - The University of Oxford awards Hawking an honorary doctorate.
  • 2004
    SOLVES THE BLACK HOLE PARADOX
    - Announcing that he has solved the Black Hole paradox, Hawking presents his recent findings at the international conference on general relativity and gravitation in Dublin.
  • 2012
    FUNDAMENTAL PHYSICS PRIZE
    - Hawking is awarded the Fundamental Physics Prize, winning $3 million for his theory on the ability for black holes to emit energy
  • 2014
    THE THEORY OF EVERYTHING
    - A film about the life of Hawking and Jane Wilde is released, staring Eddie Redmayne as Hawking.
  • 2018
    PASSES AWAY
    - Hawking's family confirmed he had died peacefully.
